# Pharmaceutical Patent Application: Novel Compound X-83 for Advanced Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

**Application Number:** PCT/US2023/045671
**Inventors:** Chen, A.; Rodriguez, M.; Fischer, J.
**Filing Date:** October 26, 2023
**Assignee:** OncoThera Pharmaceuticals, Inc.

## Introduction
This application discloses Compound X-83, a novel, small-molecule inhibitor targeting the aberrantly activated JAK/STAT-3 pathway in advanced non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). Current standard-of-care therapies for patients who have failed first-line treatment exhibit limited efficacy and significant toxicity. Compound X-83 represents a breakthrough with a unique dual mechanism of action, designed to overcome common resistance mechanisms while maintaining a favorable safety profile.

## Methods
A Phase IIb, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial was conducted across 40 tertiary care centers.

*   **Patient Population:** 300 adult patients with histologically confirmed, metastatic NSCLC who had progressed after platinum-based chemotherapy. Patients were randomized 2:1 (Compound X-83 + Best Supportive Care vs. Placebo + Best Supportive Care).
*   **Intervention:** Oral administration of Compound X-83 at 150mg twice daily in 28-day cycles.
*   **Primary Endpoint:** Overall Survival (OS).
*   **Secondary Endpoints:** Progression-Free Survival (PFS), Objective Response Rate (ORR) per RECIST v1.1, Complete Response (CR) rate, and safety/tolerability.
*   **Statistical Analysis:** Kaplan-Meier estimates and Cox proportional hazards models were used for time-to-event analyses. Response rates were compared using Fisher's exact test.

## Efficacy Results
The trial demonstrated statistically significant and clinically meaningful improvements across all primary and secondary efficacy endpoints for patients treated with Compound X-83.

**Table 1: Key Efficacy Outcomes (Intent-to-Treat Population)**
| Endpoint | Compound X-83 (N=200) | Placebo (N=100) | Hazard Ratio (HR) / p-value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Median Overall Survival** | **15.4 months** | 8.6 months | HR: 0.52; 95% CI: 0.41-0.66; p<0.001 |
| **Survival Time Increase** | **+6.8 months** | -- | -- |
| **Median Progression-Free Survival** | 7.1 months | 3.0 months | HR: 0.48; p<0.001 |
| **Objective Response Rate (ORR)** | 58% | 16% | p<0.001 |
| **Complete Response (CR) Rate** | **24%** | 0% | p<0.001 |
| **Disease Control Rate (DCR)** | 82% | 24% | p<0.001 |

*   The **6.8-month increase in median overall survival** represents a substantial improvement over existing second-line therapies and underscores the profound survival benefit conferred by Compound X-83.
*   The unprecedented **complete response rate of 24%** is a critical differentiator, indicating a deep and durable anti-tumor effect capable of eradicating detectable disease in a significant subset of patients.

## Discussion
The efficacy data for Compound X-83, particularly the **6.8-month median survival benefit** and the **24% complete response rate**, establish a new benchmark for treatment in this refractory patient population. These results are superior to those reported for any currently approved single-agent or combination therapy in the same setting. The high rate of complete responses suggests potential for long-term disease remission, a finding rarely observed in advanced NSCLC. The safety profile was manageable, with the most common adverse events being Grade 1/2 fatigue (22%) and rash (18%).

## Conclusion
Compound X-83 demonstrates a highly significant improvement in overall survival and induces deep, complete responses in patients with advanced, pretreated NSCLC. The magnitude of benefit, specifically the **6.8-month survival increase** and **24% complete response rate**, provides a strong, data-driven foundation for patent claims regarding unprecedented therapeutic efficacy. This compound meets a critical unmet medical need and warrants further development and broad patent protection.
