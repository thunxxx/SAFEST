# Clinical Trial Report: TR-1023
**A Phase III, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Neurovascor in Patients with Moderate Cognitive Impairment**

**Protocol:** NV-301-P3
**Sponsor:** Vertex Pharmaceuticals
**Date:** October 26, 2023

---

## Summary

This Phase III clinical trial evaluated the efficacy and safety of Neurovascor (50mg twice daily) compared to placebo over a 52-week period in 450 patients diagnosed with moderate cognitive impairment. The study demonstrated a **statistically significant improvement** in the primary cognitive endpoint (Cog-Assess Scale) for the Neurovascor group compared to placebo (p=0.04). The treatment was well-tolerated, with a favorable safety profile. The incidence of severe adverse events was low in the treatment group (3 patients, 1.3%) and was higher in the placebo group (8 patients, 3.6%). These results support Neurovascor as a promising therapeutic intervention for slowing cognitive decline.

---

## Methods

**Study Design:** Multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group study.
**Participants:** 450 patients aged 55-80 years with a confirmed diagnosis of moderate cognitive impairment (MCI), MMSE score 20-26.
**Intervention:** Patients were randomized 1:1 to receive either Neurovascor 50mg or matching placebo, administered orally twice daily.
**Primary Endpoint:** Change from baseline to Week 52 in the Cog-Assess Scale total score.
**Secondary Endpoints:** Changes in ADAS-Cog, Clinical Global Impression of Change (CGIC), and Quality of Life measures.
**Safety Monitoring:** Adverse events (AEs) were monitored throughout the study and graded for severity (mild, moderate, severe). Laboratory tests, vital signs, and ECGs were performed at scheduled intervals.
**Statistical Analysis:** Efficacy analyses were performed on the Intent-to-Treat (ITT) population using an ANCOVA model. Safety analyses included all randomized patients who received at least one dose of study medication.

---

## Results

### Efficacy

The primary efficacy analysis demonstrated a significant benefit of Neurovascor over placebo.
*   **Mean Change in Cog-Assess Score (SD):** Neurovascor: +4.2 points (3.1); Placebo: +1.1 points (3.5).
*   **Treatment Difference (95% CI):** 3.1 points (0.8, 5.4).
*   **p-value for efficacy:** **0.04**

Secondary endpoints showed consistent directional trends favoring Neurovascor, though not all reached statistical significance.

### Adverse Events

The overall incidence of adverse events (AEs) was similar between the two groups. Most AEs were mild to moderate in severity.

*   **Total AEs:** Neurovascor: 68%; Placebo: 65%.
*   **Treatment-related AEs:** Neurovascor: 42%; Placebo: 38%.
*   **Severe Adverse Events (SAEs):**
    *   **Neurovascor Group:** **3 patients** (1.3%) reported SAEs. These included one case of syncope, one case of acute renal impairment (resolved), and one hospitalization for pneumonia.
    *   **Placebo Group:** **8 patients** (3.6%) reported SAEs. Events included two cerebrovascular accidents, three hospitalizations for worsening cognitive/behavioral symptoms, one myocardial infarction, one case of severe gastroenteritis, and one fall with fracture.
*   **Discontinuations due to AEs:** Neurovascor: 5 patients (2.2%); Placebo: 4 patients (1.8%).
*   **Notable Lab Findings:** No clinically significant differences in hematology, chemistry, or urinalysis parameters were observed between groups.

---

## Discussion

The TR-1023 study successfully met its primary objective, showing that Neurovascor significantly improves cognitive performance compared to placebo over 52 weeks in patients with MCI. The **statistically significant p-value of 0.04** and the clinically meaningful treatment difference of 3.1 points on the Cog-Assess scale represent a substantial finding.

Importantly, the safety profile of Neurovascor was favorable. The incidence of severe adverse events was notably lower in the active treatment group (**3 events**) than in the placebo group (**8 events**), suggesting the treatment may be associated with a reduction in serious health events in this population, though this observation requires further investigation. The most common AEs in the Neurovascor group were mild headache and nausea, which were generally transient.

**Limitations:** The study duration of 52 weeks may be insufficient to assess long-term disease-modifying effects. The patient population was limited to moderate MCI, and results may not generalize to other stages of cognitive impairment.

**Conclusion:** Neurovascor demonstrates significant efficacy and a favorable safety profile in patients with moderate cognitive impairment. These robust results warrant further long-term studies and support progression towards regulatory submission.
