# Equity Research Report: Global Semiconductor Sector Outlook

**Report Date:** October 26, 2023
**Coverage:** Semiconductor Manufacturing & Equipment
**Ticker Focus:** NVDA, ASML, TSM, AMAT

## Executive Summary

We maintain an **Overweight** rating on the semiconductor sector, driven by sustained demand in AI infrastructure, automotive electrification, and inventory normalization cycles. Our 12-month price targets reflect a 15-20% upside potential for select names, with NVDA and ASML as top picks. Near-term headwinds include geopolitical supply chain constraints and cyclical memory pricing, but structural growth drivers remain intact.

## Key Investment Thesis

1.  **AI Compute Demand:** Generative AI workloads continue to drive unprecedented demand for high-performance GPUs and advanced packaging. We estimate the AI accelerator market to grow at a 35% CAGR through 2027.
2.  **Capital Expenditure Cycle:** Leading foundries and IDMs are committing to multi-year capex plans for next-generation nodes (2nm, GAA-FET), benefiting front-end equipment suppliers.
3.  **Inventory Correction Complete:** After four quarters of digestion, channel inventories have normalized across most end-markets, setting the stage for a broad-based revenue rebound in H1 2024.

## Company-Specific Updates

*   **NVIDIA Corp. (NVDA):** Price Target: $650. Data Center revenue again exceeded expectations, with visibility extending into 2024. New product ramps (H200, B100) should sustain momentum.
*   **ASML Holding NV (ASML):** Price Target: €750. EUV shipment guidance remains firm. High-NA EUV pilot tools are on track for 2024 delivery, securing technology leadership.
*   **Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM):** Price Target: $110. 3nm ramp is accelerating, with strong uptake from both smartphone and HPC customers. Geopolitical overhang persists but is priced in.
*   **Applied Materials (AMAT):** Price Target: $165. Well-positioned to capture share in ICAPS (IoT, Communications, Auto, Power, Sensors) and advanced packaging.

## Financial Model Adjustments

We have updated our models to reflect Q3 earnings. Key changes include:
- Increased NVDA FY24 EPS estimate by 8% to $12.50.
- Moderated ASML 2024 revenue growth to 12% on potential EUV timing delays.
- Maintained sector EBITDA margin forecast of ~35%.

## Risks to Our Outlook

*   **Downside Risks:** Escalation of US-China trade restrictions, a sharper-than-expected global economic slowdown, and delays in AI monetization.
*   **Upside Risks:** Faster adoption of AI at the edge, accelerated EUV adoption for DRAM, and favorable resolution of cross-strait tensions.
