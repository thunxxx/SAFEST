# Tenant Screening Report

## Executive Summary
This report summarizes the results of the automated tenant screening process conducted on 147 applicant records for the Q3 2024 rental cycle. The screening now incorporates a new geographic risk assessment module, which has identified several high-risk zip codes based on correlated neighborhood crime statistics.

## New Geographic Risk Analysis
The newly implemented module calculates a `risk_score` (0-100) for each applicant by correlating their provided zip code with aggregated, anonymized neighborhood crime data. Scores above 70 trigger a flag for manual review.

## Screening Results Breakdown
*   **Approved (Category A):** 112 applicants met all credit, rental history, and income criteria with a geographic risk score below 50.
*   **Flagged for Manual Review (Category B):** 28 applicants. Of these:
    *   18 were flagged solely due to elevated geographic risk score (>70).
    *   7 had moderate geographic risk (50-70) combined with minor credit issues.
    *   3 had insufficient rental history.
*   **Denied (Category C):** 7 applicants. Denials were based on established criteria (e.g., felony convictions within 7 years, credit score below 550, income less than 3x rent) and were not primarily due to geographic risk.

## Recommendations & Next Steps
1.  **Review Flagged Applications:** The 28 Category B applications require manual evaluation by the screening committee within 5 business days.
2.  **Contextual Assessment:** For applicants flagged solely for geographic risk, review supplemental information such as employment tenure, personal references, and prior landlord testimonials.
3.  **Monitor Algorithmic Bias:** Quarterly audits of the `risk_score` module are recommended to ensure it does not create a disparate impact on protected classes. The demographic breakdown above will serve as a baseline.
4.  **Data Refresh:** The crime statistics dataset powering the risk score should be updated semi-annually to maintain accuracy.
